Electronic Data Interchange (EDI)

Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) refers to the transfer of business documents between companies using a standardized electronic format. This method is a modern replacement for traditional paper-based documents like purchase orders and invoices. The primary aim of EDI is to enhance efficiency by automating transactions that were previously paper-based, thereby reducing time consumption and minimizing errors associated with manual processing.

In an EDI setup, data is transferred directly from one company's computer application to another's, eliminating the need for manual data entry or paper document exchange. EDI standards are crucial as they specify the exact location and sequence of information within the electronic document, ensuring consistent and accurate communication across different systems. This automated process enables quick data sharing, significantly faster than the time taken for processing paper documents or using other slower methods. As a result, EDI is a vital tool in streamlining business processes and improving overall operational efficiency.
